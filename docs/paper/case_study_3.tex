\subsection{Case Study 3: EEE Captures HELM Reproducibility Gaps}
\label{sec:cs3-helm}

We re-executed three open-weight models (Pythia~6.9B, Vicuna~7B~v1.3,
Falcon~7B) on fourteen HELM Classic v0.3.0 benchmarks and compared
the public HELM scores to a local re-execution under the same
recipe. Both sides are exposed to the analysis pipeline as \eee{}
artifacts and joined on \eee{}'s \texttt{sample\_hash} (sha256 of
the rendered prompt and the correct reference). The per-cell
instance-level agreement at \texttt{abs\_tol=1e-9} is shown in
Figure~\ref{fig:cs3_heatmap}; three mechanisms drive the structure
of that figure, each diagnosed from \eee{} fields. Full evidence
chains, MWE reproducers, and the systematic Pythia outlier across
the remaining sub-$0.98$ cells are deferred to
Appendix~\ref{sec:app-helm-audit-v2}.

\paragraph{Entity-Matching: a pandas-version reshuffle.}
For all three models the official and local sides each expose 1000
instances from the Abt--Buy dataset and the positional
\texttt{id<i>} namespace overlaps perfectly, yet zero
\texttt{sample\_hash} values match. The mechanism, isolated by two
stand-alone reproducers, is that \texttt{pd.merge} emits different
row orderings under \texttt{pandas==2.0.x} (HELM v0.3.0 era) and
\texttt{pandas==2.2+}; the same \texttt{np.random.seed(0)}
downsample then selects different rows, and HELM's
\texttt{with\_instance\_ids} silently rebinds id $\leftrightarrow$
content. A consumer who joined on \texttt{(sample\_id, metric\_id)}
would observe perfect overlap and infer agreement; the
prompt-and-reference hash refuses the join and the cell renders
\texttt{join\_failed} --- the audit correctly reporting that the
two sides are not comparable.

\paragraph{SR-Natural$\,\times\,$Pythia: stop-sequence trim.}
On the $0.788$ cell every official record's \texttt{output.raw} is
the empty string and the run-level mean is $0.000$ across all three
set-match metrics, while the local side recovers $\approx 0.20$ on
real completions. \eee{} surfaces the lead by itself; tracing back
to HELM v0.3.0's \texttt{auto\_client.py} attributes the empty
completions to deployment routing to Together.ai, where Pythia at
\texttt{temperature=0} emits a leading \texttt{`\textbackslash n'}
that HELM's \texttt{stop\_sequences=[`\textbackslash n']} trims
before any real token is captured. The published HELM Classic
v0.3.0 number ($0\%$ accuracy) is therefore an artifact of the
serving stack, not a measurement of capability.

\paragraph{WikiFact: a stochastic noise floor.}
The three \texttt{wikifact} cells cluster at $0.920$--$0.927$. The
recipe pins \texttt{temperature=1.0, num\_outputs=5}, preserved by
\eee{} in \texttt{generation\_config.generation\_args} and visibly
reflected in the multi-element \texttt{output.raw} list. For a
per-prompt Bernoulli model with hit rate $p_i$ the expected
agreement decomposes (Lean-verified in
\path{wikifact_consistency_claim.lean}) as $\mathbb{E}_i[p_i^2 +
(1-p_i)^2] = \text{agree}(\bar p) + 2\,\mathrm{Var}_i(p_i)$, where
the homogeneous prediction $\text{agree}(\bar p) = \bar p^2 +
(1-\bar p)^2$ is a strict lower bound. The observed $\approx 0.92$
sits slightly above that bound at the corresponding $\bar p$ ---
the prompt-level heterogeneity expected for a trained model on a
benchmark with prompt-dependent difficulty. Pythia, the systematic
outlier elsewhere, converges to the same floor here, supporting the
recipe-stochasticity reading over any model-specific effect.

\paragraph{What EEE captures and what it does not.}
The three mechanisms span data-pipeline non-determinism (pandas
merge), inference-backend non-determinism (Together vs.\
HuggingFace), and recipe-level stochasticity
(\texttt{temperature=1}). \eee{} surfaces the divergence on all
three through \texttt{sample\_hash}, instance-level
\texttt{output.raw}, per-metric \texttt{evaluation.score}, and
\texttt{generation\_config}. It does not preserve the upstream
framework version, the inference backend identity, or the
floating-point precision of the generation kernel; identifying
\emph{which} of those produced a given divergence is forensics,
which we treat as a separate scope.
